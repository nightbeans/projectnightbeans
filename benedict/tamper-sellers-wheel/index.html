It seems to be a common issue with a lot of eggs benedicts I am seeing recently. It's unbalanced. A good eggs benedict ‚Äì like most of life ‚Äì is about finding the right balance.

Tamper falls foul of the most typical eggs benedict failures, too much meat. A mouthful of english muffin, ham, egg and hollandaise is a unity. If one of the parts is too much, it is ruined.

The other issue is that it's not even ham*, it is a "salt-beef benedict". Why? What possible reason? Having it slow-cooked for 16 hours doesn't impress anyone. It's a gimmick.

The last thing that indicates the discomfort of Tamper with this dish is the inclusion of "Yuzu Hollandaise". Again, why? What is Yuzu adding? Hollandaise doesn't need tweaking.

I like the space, I like the atmosphere. I like being there with my wife. But I have had this eggs benedict 3 times at least. It surprises me greatly that they haven't changed it.

Unbalanced and unconfident.

*Note: I am perfectly happy with a benedict that uses bacon.
