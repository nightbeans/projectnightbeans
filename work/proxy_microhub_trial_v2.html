What the data says

Summary

The effect compounded rather than decayed — this reads as a durable demand shift, not a launch spike.

  • Combined DRR moved $0.05 → $0.08 → $0.10 across pre-trial, the 11 June review, and the last fortnight. Every week since cutover has been at or above the last.
  • Sobe Edo is the standout: from 8.7 to ~61 rentals/day and DRR $0.02 → $0.10. The hub that justified the trial is now indistinguishable from its siblings.
  • Convergence. Despite very different starting points, all three hubs now sit at ~59–61 rentals/day. With four active agents each, that is ~15 rentals/agent/day — which looks like a shared ceiling.
  • The price cut fully paid for itself in volume: rentals more than tripled combined, so revenue per battery doubled despite the lower price.

Considerations before scaling

  • Halfway to the WIG. $0.10 combined against the $0.18 target. Best single days touch $0.12–0.13, so the target is visible but not yet in reach at current structure.
  • The constraint may have shifted from price to capacity. The convergence at ~15 rentals/agent/day suggests the next lever is agent count and coverage, not further price movement. None of the three hubs is actually running five active agents.
  • Oba agent idle since 9 June — one of the five has recorded no rentals for nearly a month. Worth a field check before the trial is written up as "five-agent structure works".
  • Allowance economics now quantifiable. At ~60 rentals/day, the ₦1,000/day/agent allowance (4 agents ≈ ₦4,000) runs at roughly 22% of the hub's ₦18,000/day take — material, but far healthier than at launch volumes.
  • Seasonal overlap. The post-window now spans the wet season; a matched-control comparison would firm up how much of the June climb is the trial vs the season.
