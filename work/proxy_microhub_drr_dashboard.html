What the data says

Summary

Demand at these hubs is price-elastic, and the trial bears that out.

The ₦400 customer price reduced MOPO's per-rental take by roughly 30%, but rental volume more than doubled, so both revenue and DRR increased.

Sobe Edo showed the largest uplift, from 8.7 to ~39 rentals/day, with DRR rising $0.02 → $0.07. Ute now holds the highest absolute DRR at $0.09. Oba moved least (+19%), having been the strongest performer of the three before the trial.

Early-June volumes are among the highest in the window, suggesting the effect is sustained rather than a launch-period spike.

Considerations before scaling

Still below the WIG. $0.08 combined is a clear improvement but remains less than half the $0.18 target.

Allowance economics untested. The ₦1,000/day/agent transport allowance is a real cost against the ₦300 take and warrants a contribution-margin check per hub.

Structure only partly in place. Active agents doubled from two at each hub, and per-agent inventory roughly halved (~57 → ~32 batteries). Oba reached the intended five agents; Sobe and Ute are at four. The redistribution also began around 20–27 May, a few days ahead of the price change.

4-week review ~21 Jun will indicate whether the trend holds.
